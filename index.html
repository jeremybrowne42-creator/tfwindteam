Designing the future of floating offshore wind.

TFwind is a student-led engineering team developing SeaGimbal – an experimental floating platform that keeps small-scale wind turbines stable in waves while maximising power output.

SeaGimbal Concept

Our platform uses a pendulum-like keel and modular structure to reduce turbine pitch motion and improve energy capture, while remaining easy to build, transport, and test at small scale.

  • Student-designed from first principles
  • Optimised for wave-tank testing
  • Focus on manufacturability & cost

Our mission

We bring together students from across Trinity to learn by doing: designing, building, and testing floating wind technology that can play a role in Ireland’s clean-energy future.

Learn by building

TFwind is a practical classroom. Members gain experience in hydrodynamics, structures, electrical systems, and controls by working on a real hardware project.

Prototype new ideas

We explore novel concepts for floating platforms, tower structures, and load-reduction strategies, then validate them in simulation and in the wave basin.

Grow a community

Our team is a community of engineers, scientists, and business-minded students passionate about offshore wind and climate solutions.

SeaGimbal: our competition platform

SeaGimbal is a small-scale floating platform designed for the Floating Wind Challenge. The goal: keep the turbine as steady as possible in realistic wave conditions while remaining easy to manufacture with student-friendly tools and budgets.

  • Three-column platform with retractable keel
  • Ball-joint pendulum to damp pitch motion
  • Modular design for rapid assembly, repair, and iteration
  • Designed for testing in Ireland’s ocean wave basins

Meet the team

TFwind is made up of undergraduate and postgraduate students from mechanical, civil, electrical, computer science, and business disciplines – all united by a shared interest in offshore wind.

Student-led & multidisciplinary

We organise ourselves into sub-teams covering structures, hydrodynamics, electrical systems, data, and operations. Team members rotate roles to gain broad experience.

Skills you’ll develop

  • Hands-on design, fabrication, and testing
  • Numerical modelling & data analysis
  • Technical communication & public outreach
  • Project management & teamwork
